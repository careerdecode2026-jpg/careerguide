2.1 Should You Appear for JEE Advanced?

Always attempt JEE Advanced if you qualify. Once you are in the top 2,50,000 in JEE Main, appearing for JEE Advanced costs nothing extra. Even if you don't get a top IIT, the experience and the possibility of a newer IIT seat makes it worth attempting every time.

2.3 Qualifying Cutoff — To Get a Rank

Scoring above these marks earns you a rank in JEE Advanced. Having a rank only means you are eligible for IIT seat allocation — actual admission depends on your rank vs. college cutoffs.

Category (Rank List)Min Total (2025)Min Per Subject% of Max
CRL (General/Open)74 marksMin 7 per subject~35%
OBC-NCL / EWS66 marksMin 6 per subject~31.5%
SC / ST / PwD37 marksMin 4 per subject~17.5%
Qualifying cutoff only gives you a rank. To get a good IIT branch, you typically need 150–220+ marks. Aim for 200+ for top IITs (CSE/EE).
